Set Up Quantum + Find Your Q-Pulse

Tips for New Techs

Jun.29, 2022

Finding and training new Technicians is one of the greatest challenges any maintenance shop faces. This is especially true now that we've collectively experienced the great resignation in our industry.

It can be difficult to find experienced technicians. Furthermore, training technicians is a lengthy, ongoing process. There's never a shortage of new things to learn.

Luckily, AJWT is a great place to learn. We're lucky to have so many senior technicians willing to train The Next Generation of technicians.

Although soon the way we interact with Quantum will most likely change (thanks to the Quantum Web App being developed), I thought it would be a good idea to share some of the Quantum tricks I've picked up since I started at AJWT.

I've compiled a short list of tricks and tips that my colleagues have passed on to me. Now, I pass those tricks to you.

Patrick's Hat Trick

@Patrick Fregeau taught me these invaluable rules when I first started at AJWT.

These are the three things you, as a technician, need to verify before starting work on a component:

  1. Verify that the Part Number and the Serial Number on the Unit match the Part Number and the Serial Number on the paperwork?
  2. Verify that we have the correct CMM?
  3. Verify that we have the correct tools?

OK, Let's break this down.

Verifying Part Number and Serial Number

Let's start by opening Quantum. Here's a screenshot of the desktop shortcut. It looks like a gear — for all the gearheads.

To get any work done, you need to select the 'Live Database'.

Once Quantum has finished loading, open 'Shop Control'. You have an option: Either press the 'Shop Control' icon at the top of the screen (indicated in pic below) or press Ctrl + 9:

Press 'F' and then type the Work Order number into the top field: (Note: the last WO you worked on will populate Shop Control by default).

Press 'Enter'. You should now see your Work Order with some tasks.

Next, read the Part Number directly from the Unit's ID plate. Now verify it against these documents:

  • The Repair Order
  • The Removal Tag - Note: the removal tag will also have information regarding the serviceable unit that replaced the unserviceable currently in front of you (you can ignore the info on that serviceable unit). Also, make a note of the reason for removal. In this case INOP. is short for inoperable.
  • WO in Quantum

Note: if your unit is missing its ID plate, as long as the Part Number and Serial Number on the Removal Tag match the Repair Order and Quantum, you have enough information. If you have no ID plate and no Removal Tag, it will be up to your cell lead to ask a Customer Service Manager to contact the unit's customer.

Verify Correct CMM and Tools

Currently, at AJWT, we keep all our documents in Q-Pulse.

First, open Q-Pulse, then expand the 'Documents' section and search by Part Number (or, if you know the ATA, you can search by the ATA number)

A new window will pop up, and you should see a list of all the documents pertaining to that unit.

To start work, you need have these two documents:

  • TCMM - the Component Maintenance Manual
  • TTE - the Tool Equivalency (Tool List)

If you at least have those two documents, you can start work on your unit.

Note: sometimes you'll also have a TCRM - Component Repair Manual.

More than likely you'll have other documents associated with your unit. These are the most common:

  • TEI - Engineering Instructions which contain special instructions from engineering (which might include alternates, special repair instructions, etc)
  • TJCL - Engineering Justification Checklist is the engineer's justification for creating the TEI
  • TAD - Airworthiness Directive documents a known safety deficiency with a particular model of aircraft that must be corrected (you must comply with the instructions in these documents)
  • TSB - Service Bulletin documents improvements that operators can choose to comply with the improve the functionality of a unit (these are optional - up to customer's discretion)

You may also see:

  • TCSI - Customer Special Instructions (check) are documents related to a specific customer's additional instructions related to the maintenance of the unit. For example: Delta Pubs

Peter's Powerful Quantum Layout

@Peter Demeter is my cell lead. Peter has taught me most of what I know about Quantum.

When I first started, he showed me how to set up Quantum. I've customized my layout since then. However, it's still very close to the layout Peter showed me three years ago.

Shop Control Parts Management (Ctrl + 9)

In Quantum, you should still have 'Shop Control' open from the last step. Navigate to the 'Parts Management' Tab.

You'll have a bunch of columns with Part Numbers, Part Description, etc....

You can customize these columns so the information you want to see is exactly where you want to see it.

I have mine set up like this (from left to right):

  • Part Number
  • Part Description
  • Cond Lev (check)
  • Figure
  • Sub-WO Number
  • Remarks
  • Disposition
  • Unit Price
  • Cond
  • Qnty Needed
  • Qnty Res (check)
  • Qnty Issued
  • Part Qnty OH
  • Part Qnty Ava (check)
  • Alt On Hand
  • Alt Available
  • Task
  • Activity
  • PO
  • Next Delivery Date
  • RO Number
  • Qnty Turned In

Personally, I don't have any reason to bother with the rest of the columns.

To customize, all you do is click and drag the columns where you want. You can also resize. Once you're happy with the way things look, just right click the table and select 'Save to personal settings'. (If you don't have time for that, just press Ctrl + Alt + S.)

Customize this screen as necessary to serve your specific needs, then 'Save to personal preferences.'

Inventory Control (Ctrl + 1)

When I'm not working in 'Shop Control', I'm usually in the 'Inventory Control' module doing a little research on specific Part Numbers.

There's also a lot of information on this screen. Luckily, we can also customize the layout.

As a technician, I'm interested in nine screens to when I'm looking up a Part Number:

  • Work Order BOM History
  • Repair Order History
  • Stock Transaction Detail
  • Purchase Order History
  • Alternate/Cross Reference Detail
  • Current Stock Reservations
  • Stock Summary
  • Work Order Main Component History
  • Stock Line History Detail

To customize this screen, right-click at the top of one of the 'browses' (any 'browses' heading will do) and click 'Configure Browses'.

Select 3 rows and 3 columns

Drag and drop to put your 'browses' where you want

When you're happy with how your 'Inventory Control', click 'OK'.

There you go. Now you can research Part Numbers in Quantum like a boss!

Customizing Buttons in your Quantum Nav

Most techs will only use a few modules. There's no sense in having a bunch of buttons visible that you'll never use. To customize your navigation, click the small button to the right of all the large buttons (where indicated), and then click 'Add or Remove Buttons'.

Personally, I only use:

  • Inventory Control
  • Repair Orders
  • Shop Control
  • Time & Attendance

However, you can customize to suite your needs.

Jo's Costing / Finalizing Checklist

@Jizhou Yu gave me this checklist when he was training me years ago, and I've been using it ever since.

I add to this checklist whenever I come across something new, or when I need to remind myself of something that I easily forget. There's only so much space in my head!

== Before Sending Unit to 'Costing', please verify ==

  • Labour hours added to tasks you worked on
  • Tasks have notes on them to say what you've done - also add name, EMPLOYEE #, and date
    • 'UNIT ASSEMBLED: READY FOR TEST. Chandler Bing 0123, Jun.29, 2022'
  • Check for ADs and SBs to see if any apply to unit
  • Upload pictures to Quantum: Damage pics / Missing Parts pics
  • Ensure 'missing' IPC is scanned (if you have missing parts, include scan of CMM indicating that missing parts are required as per CMM)
  • Edit Header (G E) => quoted for: insp/test, repair, OH - if warranted indicated, update: We Resp or Cust Resp, etc....
  • Ensure 'PN out' is correct (e.g.: carrying out an SB may change PN) => edit in Header (G E)
  • Close tasks (only if you're qualified and have SCA)
  • Ensure Tear Down / Inspection Report is written => ensure CMM is referenced in Faults Section (under Repair) (***DO NOT reference TEIs on Tear Down)

== Before Closing WO & Signing Maintenance Release, please verify ==

  • Final Assembly is complete
  • 2 AJWT stickers attached to unit
  • Lockwire is complete
  • AJWT 'heatshrinks' applied to lockwire (as necessary)
  • 'Final Pics' are uploaded to Quantum (finalizing unit)
  • Test Papers are uploaded to Quantum
  • Labour hours are added to tasks
  • Edit Header (G E) => quoted for: insp/test, repair, OH - if warranted indicated, update: We Resp or Cust Resp, etc....
  • Edit Header (G E) => under 'Miscellaneous' (for 'Block 12' on Form One)- ensure current revision of CMM is referenced
    • 'This unit was repaired in accordance with CMM __-__-__, Rev. xx, Dated: xxx xx/xx.'
    • 'For more information, please refer to the Tear Down / Inspection Report'
  • Check for Times and Cycles of unit (G E) - for Time Life units, include times (TSN, CSN | TSR, CSR | TSO, CSO) in 'Block 12' of Form One
  • Check Q-Pulse for TCSI (Customer Special Instructions)
  • Ensure Tear Down / Inspection Report is finished (***DO NOT reference TEIs on Tear Down)
  • Issue all parts (M I A S)
  • Airbus Flight Controls: place desiccant with unit in shipping bag - just before bringing unit to shipping
  • Check for BLUE shipping-box tag (if you have a BLUE shipping-box tag, attach to Form One / Tear Down Package)
  • Ensure GREEN Serviceable tag is attached to unit before bringing unit to shipping

I like to put this list on a Sticky Note so I can easily pull it up anytime I need to refer to it.

In the Search Bar at the bottom of your screen, type: "Sticky Notes".

Open the Sticky Notes App.

Next, open this:

  • TechnicianChecklist_Costing+Finalizing_French
  • TechnicianChecklist_Costing+Finalizing_English

Copy-Paste the list into a Sticky Note.

Then, if you pin "Sticky Notes" to your taskbar, the list will be right there whenever you need to refer to it.
