eVTOLs

Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing

Jun.08, 2022

Have you seen these new fancy 'flying cars'? Personally, I can't wait to climb into one of these. This would save me from my commute — my middle finger is really tired from driving in Montréal traffic. Actually, I don't drive that way. I used to. But something happened that changed my mind about driving, forever.

When I first started working, I bought myself a new car. For a moment, I was on top of the world; I had a great job, a new car, and a very sexy girlfriend. However, one day when I was driving to pick up my girlfriend for a date, I had a little accident with my new car.

You see, there was this awkward intersection with a lot of traffic. I was just fresh out of driver's training, and I was driving a new car. So, I didn't want a single scratch. I was being careful. However, there was this impatient guy behind me who had other plans. He started honking at me to "Get on with it!" I thought he was a jerk, so I showed him my middle finger. Obviously, this didn't fix anything. It only served to enrage the other driver.

Before I knew it, there was a break in traffic, and I made my turn. The other guy turned with me. I thought, "This is interesting." I made a couple of more turns, and the other guy was still behind me. I thought, "Oh, great! This guy is following me." I turned onto my girlfriend's street. The guy was still behind me. So, I pulled up to my girlfriend's house and then turned my head to see what the other guy was doing. He was turning around because he saw me pulling up to a house. I only saw the back of his head, but I could clearly see that the other guy was my girlfriend's dad! (It was unmistakeable, I recognized his car immediately.)

He drove away (I think he thought I didn't see him). I rang the doorbell to my girlfriend's house, and, when she came to the door I, confessed, "I think I just gave your dad the finger." She wasn't very impressed with me, and the date didn't go very well.

Just think how much better that date would have gone if I had taken my flying car. I would have flown right over that stupid intersection, and avoided all that tension and embarrassment. The moral of the story is: driving in traffic is slow, frustrating, and messy. Why drive in traffic when you can fly over it?

Also, the other angry driver is now my father-in-law. I'm happy to report we're friends now.

There are a lot of brains and cash involved

I can't tell you which eVTOLs (pronounced EE-VEE-tolz) are going to become the next big commercial success. However, I can tell you that some of us will probably be flying one of these new eVTOLs in the near future. At the 2022 VFS Nikolsky Lecture, Professor Marilyn J. Smith, Director of the Vertical Lift Research Center of Excellence (VLRCOE) at the Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta, commented that there are more than 600 distinct Unmanned Aerial Vehicles and Advanced Air Mobility aircraft designs listed on the Vertical Flight Society's eVTOL Aircraft Directory. Also, Professor Marilyn Smith pointed out that the Advanced Air Mobility Reality Index lists 5 to 6 billion dollars in funding for these vehicles.

That is an absolute explosion of innovation!

If you don't believe that some of us will be flying one of these new eVTOLs in the near future, check out Jetson's website. If you scroll down the page, you'll see that you can order your own personal electric aerial vehicle:

To order your Jetson, a down payment of $22,000 is required. A final payment of $70,000 when your Jetson ONE is ready for delivery at the factory.

Jetson is fully booked with orders for their personal electric vehicles until the end of next year! The last time I checked, there were only three more chassis available for 2023 (chassis #168, #169, and #170).

Lilium

This is another very exciting eVTOL project that is making a name for itself. I'm starting to see pictures of the Lilium Jet everywhere given its attractive design. It's quickly becoming the poster child of this new eVTOL phenomenon.

Lilium is hard at work making the Lilium Jet: a 7-Seater eVTOL with a maximum range of 300km (186 miles), assuming that better battery performance capabilities are achieved by the year 2025 and flight conditions are ideal.

Check out this 11-minute video for a very interesting look at the engineering that is going into the making of the Lilium Jet.

Bellwether

Based out of London, England, Bellwether Industries is diligently working to create a sleek eVTOL aircraft that promises to "...build a brand new 3-Dimensional lifestyle and to lead the world toward a more comprehensive urban mobility."

Bellwether's first project, Volar, has room for one person (who is also the pilot). The plan is to sell the Volar as a "Private Urban Aircraft for Intracity Travel." If you haven't seen pictures of the Volar yet, do yourself and your eyes a favour. Check out their website: Bellwether Industries. It is a gorgeous aircraft!
