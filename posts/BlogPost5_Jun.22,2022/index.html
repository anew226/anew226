Keyboard Shortcuts Cheat Sheet

Downloadable Cheat Sheet for Keyboard Shortcuts

Jun.22, 2022

For all you keyboard shortcuts enthusiasts: here's a quick reference for keyboard shortcuts I use at AJWT every day.

When looking at the cheat sheet, all the shortcuts on the left — Windows, Word, and Adobe — you will need to press the keys at same time. However, all the shortcuts on the right — Quantum — you need to press the keys one after the other.

There are two exceptions:

  • Adobe Reader - Alt W then N
    • Press 'Alt W' together to access Window from top menu
    • Then press 'N' to open current document in a New Window
  • Quantum - Ctrl Alt →
    • Press Ctrl Alt → together to toggle the previous screen

Quantum Shortcuts Primer

In Quantum, you can use keyboard shortcuts to access most menus and menu options. If you find yourself repeatedly accessing the same menus, you might want to take a quick note. There might be a faster way to get there.

You can create your own shortcuts! Just pay attention to the Underlined letters.

For example: as a technician, we frequently navigate to 'Tear Down/Inspection Report'. If you were to use your mouse, you would click on 'Global' to access the Global menu. Then, you would click on 'Print' to access the Print sub-menu. Finally, you would click on 'Tear Down/Inspection Report' to access the print window for your Tear Down.

If you look closely, you'll see that 'G' in Global is underlined, and that the 'P' in Print is also underlined. Finally, a little curve ball, it's the 'I' in Inspection that is underlined. So, instead of using your mouse to click, click, click, you can use your keyboard to access the menus. Press 'G' on your keyboard to access Global, then press 'P' to access Print, and then press 'I' to access Tear Down/Inspection Report.

Try it out. I'm sure you'll find this much faster.
